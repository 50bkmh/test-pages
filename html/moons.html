What is a Moon?

Planets and asteroids orbit the Sun. Moons—also known as natural satellites—orbit planets and asteroids. Moons come in many shapes, sizes and types. Most are airless, but a few have atmospheres and even hidden oceans. There are hundreds of moons in our solar system—even a few asteroids have small companion moons.There are hundreds of moons in our solar system—even a few asteroids have been found to have small companion moons. Earth's Moon

The brightest and largest object in our night sky, the Moon makes Earth a more livable planet by moderating our home planet's wobble on its axis, leading to a relatively stable climate.

Mars Moons

Like Earth's Moon, Phobos and Deimos always present the same face to their planet. They are among the darker objects in the solar system. The moons appear to be made of carbon-rich rock mixed with ice and may be captured asteroids.

Jupiter Moons

Moons swarm around this giant world, including icy Europa with its hidden ocean. Jupiter has 79 moons. There are many interesting moons orbiting the planet, but the ones of most scientific interest are the Galilean satellites.

Saturn Moons

Some moons of Saturn shape its beautiful rings. The Voyager and Pioneer flybys provided rough sketches of Saturn’s moons. But NASA’s Cassini spacecraft discovered previously unknown moons, solved mysteries about known ones

Uranus Moons

While most of the satellites orbiting other planets take their names from ancient mythologies, Uranus' moons are unique in being named for Shakespearean characters.
